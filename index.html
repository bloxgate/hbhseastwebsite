The Golden Question: What is EAST?

EAST is a project-based, service-learning oriented program that provides students with high-end technology available in the most progressive fields in the world. At its heart, EAST is a coordinated effort to provide todayâ€™s students with an educational atmosphere that allows them to gain insight into their own abilities to acquire and use information, solve problems and develop valuable experience. Since its inception in 1996, the EAST model has expanded to over 200 schools in five states.

Career Opportunities

EAST gives students the skills and expertise necessary to succeed in the real world. Students learn professinalism, video editing, and other skills that they could take into their career. These skills are applied to their projects in a way that challenges the student to think on a technical level about their project. Students take on a goal, and they stick with that goal until either they fail, or they succeed. One of the Mottos at Har-Ber EAST is "Failure is okay", because ultimately, we learn from failure, as people do in their career field.

Community Involvement

Community involvement is one of the major pillars in EAST. Every project must incorporate a partner (usually local buisnesses, friends of the EAST Program, or other faculty members.) This is where the students learn to apply the skills they have learned in a meaningful and practical way.
An EAST Project cannot suceed without community involvement.

Student Growth

EAST is not a traditional classroom. Students are the teachers. Facilitators maintain the program, and provide guidance for the students, but they don't necessarily do the projects for the students. Students teach themselves anything they want to learn that will hep them further their project.
